Power Greed and Truth

I had been without sleep, traveling for 2 days from Toronto Canada to Riga Latvia. My cousin Gunta, state chancellor at the time, was taking me from the airport to my final destination. Exhausted, barely able to speak in English, yet alone in Latvian, I still remember crystal clear her first words to me. Translated, "Don't listen to the media. I don't even understand what they are talking about, and I am doing the job. If you want to know what is going on in government, ask me, and I will tell you right away." I did as she asked. Over the years I noticed that when the know-it-alls are most concerned about things, it is business as usual in the government; yet when there is internal crisis, the know-it-alls are completely unaware, by choice. I knew the internal truth, and sat alone silently, as no one else wanted to know. It was such a bizarre way of living. The truth in administration and the perceived truth of the people for which the administration serves. 

"Power and greed", continues Gunta (translated), "People who thrive on power and greed are attracted to governments because they have most money and power. This is the biggest battle of governments; the ongoing process of weeding these people out." Indeed, her first year as chancellor, she fired all Kremlin staff, and the second year the fat cats. SHE did it. The female. It is the females doing the most courageous things. The things to protect the whole, that have scary consequences. That is why it is so important for men to PROTECT and support women, not control and destroy them. When men lose their freedom at the hands of other men, their only chance to regain it is through women. At least historically, that has repeated itself, in Latvia, in the world.
