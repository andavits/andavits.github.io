COMMUNCATION & DECISION-MAKING
communcation
  • Data analysis for end users is communicated through reports (pdfs/presetnations), dashboards (shiny)
  • Communcation amongst analysts is done with special documents that can embed scripts and math symbols.
decision-making
Science does
  • Behavior statistical results have precise numerics explaining patterns between varaibles and our confidence in these results.
Science does not
  • Science is not designed to answer questions of what to do with the results. Science simply tries to explain phenomena with maths, building on what hasbeen done in the past. Science reviews the process to create the results, but does not pass judgement about results itself, which is required to make a decision of what to do with the results in a practical sense.
Maths for Decisions
    Mathematics that determine the probability that something will happen in the future based on explicitly defined dataset. Bayes theory considers the uncertainty mathematics in science using the maths of probabilities.
Machine Learning for Decisions
  • Computers are great for large complex calculations beyond initial analysis. There are many ways to do this, so it is important for end user to understand the gist of how the particular process works. Make sure it is transparent. Black boxed results are great for leads on what to do, but not intended to make final conclusions.
  • It is important to consider the validity of the datasets being used. Are they indeed representing the phenomenon accurately. This is especially important whenthe consequences can be negative, like recidivism, credit score, employment opportunities etc.
Impact v Program Success
  • Simpson's Paradox: can pass program, but no impact
  • e.g. Pass anger management program, but no impact because living in oppressive/abusive environment. Such as Black person in community where police are required to recruit inmates for private prisons, or child in home, neglected/abused by narcissitic mother projecting own issues...
