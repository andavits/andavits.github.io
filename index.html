STATISTICS - THE SCIENCE OF DATA
Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, and interpreting numerical facts, which we call data. Data are numbers with context. In practice, any set of data is accompanied by background information that helps us understand the data. When starting new project, the following are the first questions we ask:
  • why important: why?
    • What purpose does the data have?
    • Do we hope to answer some specific questions?
    • Do we want to draw conclusions about individuals other than those for whom we actually have the data?
  • what's it about? who?
    • What individuals do the data describe?
    • How many individuals appear in the data?
  • details? what?
    • How many variables do the data contain?
    • What are the exact definitions of these variables? In what unit of measure is each variable recorded?
