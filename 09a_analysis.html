HEXACO
We all know of the "big 5" trait analysis that is actively used with big data for marketing and influence platforms. HEXACO adds another factor to describe our traits; which is a person's motive to lie. Created by scientists here in Canada, it successfully addresses the weakest part of the Big 5.
  • How to Spot a Dishonest Person: The role of honesty-humility in personality
  • How Honest Are You About Your Religion?: A summary of chapter 8 of 'The H-Factor of Personality'
Big 5 (aka OCEAN model)
In this set of analysis, I became the devil's advocate, and tried to disprove that we have 5 traits to describe our personality; because, on the surface, it seems like an arbitrary classification. Turns out, it is not happen-stance at all. I tried various dimension reduction methods, and the results verify the strength of these categories. The weakest correlation is now handled with the HEXACO extensione of Big5.

Grocery shopping behaviour
IF Charlie purchases milk and eggs THEN it is likely that he will purchase cheese. This is called Association analysis and it answers IF-THEN questions. A useful result from this type of analysis for management, is the visual tree that can be created, showing exactly how a decision is made.
